1.0 Introduction
Blockchain  technology  has become increasingly important in modern digital systems due to
its  ability  to  provide  secure,  transparent,  and  decentralized  transaction  management.
Blockchain technology is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions securely and
transparently.  Unlike  traditional  systems  that  rely  on  a  central  authority,  blockchain  stores
information  in  interconnected  blocks  that  cannot  be  easily  modified  once  recorded.  This
technology  provides  several  important  features  such as transparency, security, immutability,
and decentralization (Tapscott & Tapscott, 2016).

In  recent  years,  blockchain  technology  has  been  applied  in  various  industries
including  finance,  healthcare,  supply  chain  management,  and digital payment systems. One
of its most valuable features is the use of smart contracts, which are self-executing programs
that automatically perform actions when predefined conditions are met (Buterin, 2014). This
helps reduce the need for intermediaries and improves the efficiency of transactions.

In  the  freelance  industry,  trust  issues  often  exist  between  clients  and  freelancers.
Freelancers  may  experience  delayed  payments or non-payment after completing their work,
while  clients  may  worry  about  receiving  poor-quality  deliverables  after  making  payments.
These challenges often lead to disputes and reduce trust between both parties.

To  address  these  issues,  this  project  proposes  MilestonePay,  a  blockchain-based
freelance  payment  platform  that  uses  smart  contracts  and  an  escrow  mechanism.  The
platform  allows  clients  to  securely  deposit  funds  before  a  project  begins,  while freelancers
receive  payments  automatically  after  milestone  approval.  Smart  contracts  ensure  that
payment conditions are enforced fairly and transparently. By utilizing blockchain technology,
MilestonePay  aims  to  improve  transparency,  security,  trust,  and  efficiency  throughout  the
project lifecycle.

This  project  explores  how  blockchain  technology  can  be  applied  to  the  freelance
industry  through the development of MilestonePay and evaluates its potential benefits using
the Business Model Canvas (BMC) framework.

The objectives of this project are:

●  To develop a secure blockchain-based escrow payment system.
●  To automate milestone payments using smart contracts.
●  To improve transparency between clients and freelancers.
●  To reduce payment disputes and fraud.
●  To provide a fair payment process for both parties.

2.0 Business Model Canvas (BMC) Overview

The  Business  Model  Canvas  (BMC)  for  MilestonePay  outlines  a  strategic  framework
designed  to  eliminate  the  inherent  trust  deficit  between independent freelancers and remote
clients. Rather than relying on a centralized intermediary that maintains absolute control over
user  accounts  and  transaction  records,  this  business  model  structures  a  decentralized,
peer-to-peer  execution  network.  The  operational  architecture  maps  out  how  value  is
co-created, secured, and distributed across a two-sided market without exposing participants
to  payment anxieties or project abandonment risks. By optimizing this layout, MilestonePay
directly  coordinates  technical  resources,  decentralized  storage  protocols,  and  automated
payment channels into a self-sustaining financial ecosystem.

3.0 Industry Analysis
3.1 Overview of the Freelance Industry
The freelance industry has become increasingly popular with the growth of remote work and
digital  platforms.  Today,  many  clients  hire  freelancers  for  services  such  as  programming,
graphic  design,  content  writing,  and  digital  marketing.  These  platforms  allow  people  from
different countries to work together more easily.

Despite  these  advantages,  several  problems  still  exist.  Clients  and  freelancers  often  do  not
know each other before starting a project, which can lead to trust issues. Freelancers may face
delayed  payments  or  non-payment,  while  clients  may  worry  about  receiving  poor-quality
work.  As  a  result,  disputes  regarding  payments,  project  requirements,  and work quality are
common in the freelance industry.

3.2 Current Problems in Freelance Payments
One  of  the  major  issues  in  freelance  marketplaces  is  the  lack  of  trust  between  clients  and
freelancers.

Delayed Payments
Freelancers  frequently  face  delays  in  receiving  their  money,  as  centralized  platforms  often
hold funds in escrow for lengthy periods before releasing them.

High Commissions and Service Fees
Centralized  freelancing  platforms  act  as  intermediaries  and  impose  high  service  charges,
which significantly reduce the final earnings that freelancers receive for their work.

Fraud and Scams
There are persistent risks of fraud in freelance transactions, including scams where attackers
create  fake  accounts  to  secure  advance  payments  without  completing  the required tasks, or
exploit vulnerabilities to manipulate refund mechanisms.

Disputes and Unfair Resolutions
Disputes  regarding  work  verification  and  contract  violations  are  common,  and  traditional
platforms  often  resolve  these  conflicts  in  a  manner  that  favors  the  platform  over  its  users,
frequently resulting in monetary losses for freelancers.

Withheld Payments and Account Restrictions
Because  centralized  platforms  maintain  full  control  over  the  system,  they  can  arbitrarily
censor  users,  suspend  accounts  without  obvious  cause,  and  withhold  payments  from
freelancers.

3.3 Blockchain-Based Solution: MilestonePay
MilestonePay  is  a  blockchain-based  platform  designed  to  improve  the  payment  process
between  clients  and  freelancers. The platform uses smart contracts and an escrow system to
create a more secure and transparent working environment.

In MilestonePay, clients first create a project and divide it into several milestones. Before the
project  starts,  the  client  deposits  the  full  payment  amount  into  the  platform.  The  funds are
then locked in a smart contract and will only be released when the agreed conditions are met.

As the freelancer completes each milestone, the completed work is submitted for review. The
client  checks  whether  the  work  meets  the  project  requirements.  Once  the  milestone  is
approved,  the  smart  contract  automatically  releases  the  corresponding  payment  to  the
freelancer. This process continues until all milestones have been completed and approved.

If  a  disagreement  occurs between the client and freelancer, the issue can be escalated to the
platform administrator or an appointed arbitrator. They will review the evidence provided by
both parties and make a decision based on the project agreement and platform policies. This
helps ensure that disputes are handled fairly and transparently.

3.4 Benefits of Blockchain Implementation

Automated and Secure Payments
By utilizing smart contracts, blockchain platforms can automate escrow services and payment
distribution.  Funds  are  securely  locked  at  the  start  of  a  project  and  are  only  released
automatically  when  predefined  conditions  or  milestones  are  met,  ensuring  freelancers  are
paid reliably and on time.

Lower Transaction Fees
Traditional  freelance  platforms  act  as  intermediaries  and  charge  high  commissions.
Blockchain  removes  the  need  for  these  centralized  middlemen,  which  significantly reduces
service fees and administration costs, allowing freelancers to retain their full earnings.

Enhanced Transparency and Trust
Blockchain provides a transparent and secure way to record transactions and agreements. All
transaction records are stored permanently on the blockchain and cannot be easily changed or
deleted.  This  allows  both  clients  and  freelancers  to  view  the  project  and  payment  history,
helping to build trust between both parties and reducing the risk of fraud or disputes.

Decentralization and User Control
Since  blockchain  operates  on  a  decentralized network, no single party has complete control
over  the  system.  This  reduces  the  risk  of  unfair  actions  such  as  account  restrictions,
unauthorized  changes,  or  withholding  payments.  Users  have  greater  control  over  their
transactions and project information.

reliable.

3.5 Challenges of Implementation

Smart Contract Security Risks
Smart contracts may contain coding errors or security vulnerabilities if they are not properly
tested. Since smart contracts cannot be easily modified after deployment, any mistakes could
affect the payment process or be exploited by attackers.

Scalability and Transaction Costs
Public  blockchain  networks  may  experience  network  congestion  when  many  transactions
occur at the same time. This can lead to slower transaction processing and higher transaction
fees, which may affect the efficiency of the platform.

Legal and Regulatory Challenges
Blockchain  technology  and  smart  contracts  are  still  relatively  new  in  many countries. As a
result,  there  may  be  legal  and  regulatory  uncertainties  regarding  digital  contracts,  payment
processing, and dispute resolution.

3.6 Real-World Relevance
Blockchain technology is already being used in many industries, including finance, logistics,
and  digital  payment  systems.  In  the  freelance  industry,  platforms  such  as  Ethlance  and
LaborX use blockchain and smart contracts to improve payment security and transparency.

Ethlance  is  a  blockchain-based  freelancing  platform  that  connects  clients  and  freelancers
without  relying  heavily  on  a  central  authority.  The  platform  uses  blockchain  technology to
provide transparent transactions and allows users to interact directly with each other.

LaborX  is  another  blockchain-powered  freelancing  platform  that  uses  smart  contracts  to
automate agreements and payments. It also supports escrow services, ensuring that payments
are only released when agreed conditions have been fulfilled.

These  platforms  demonstrate  that  blockchain  technology  can  be  successfully  applied in the
freelance industry. Similar to Ethlance and LaborX, MilestonePay aims to improve trust and
transparency  between  clients  and  freelancers  through  the  use  of  blockchain  and  smart
contracts.  However,  MilestonePay  focuses specifically on milestone-based payments, where
funds  are  released  gradually  as  project  milestones  are  completed  and  approved.  This
approach  helps reduce payment disputes, protects both parties, and ensures a fairer payment
process  throughout  the  project  lifecycle.  As  freelancing and remote work continue to grow,
solutions  such  as  MilestonePay  can  provide  a  more  secure  and  efficient  way  to  manage
freelance projects and payments.

4.0 BMC Development
4.1 Customer Segments
The  MilestonePay  platform  targets  two  distinct  but  interdependent  primary  user  classes  (a
two-sided market) and one internal managing segment. B2B (Business-to-Business) and B2C
(Business-to-Consumer)  describe  who  a company sells to. B2B sells products or services to
other companies, while B2C sells directly to individual end-users.

Freelancers / Independent Contractors (B2C & B2B)
Individual  software  developers,  UI/UX  designers,  content  writers,  and  creative  consultants
who  suffer  from  cash  flow  instability.  Their  primary  pain  point  is  the  risk  of  client
non-payment or delayed payouts after work completion.

Clients / Small Businesses / Startups (B2B)
Project owners and companies seeking external talent but fearing sunk costs on incomplete or
low-quality deliverables. They require financial protection before releasing capital.

Platform Administrators / Arbitrators (Internal & Supporting)
While  not  external  buyers,  they  are  a  segment  that  interacts  heavily  with  the  ecosystem  to
manage  platform  integrity.  They  are  also  in  charge  of  resolving  milestone  disputes  and
overseeing escrow anomalies.

4.2 Value Propositions
MilestonePay  leverages  blockchain  and  Solidity  smart  contracts  to  deliver  trustless,
decentralized security to both sides of the freelance market.

For Freelancers (Guaranteed Payment & Trust)
By implementing MilestonePay, the freelancers are able to guarantee their payment and trust
to eliminate their "payment anxiety." Once a client deposits funds into the immutable escrow
contract,  the  freelancer  has  cryptographic  proof  that  the  money  exists  and  is  locked.
Milestone  completion  triggers  automated  and  instant  payouts  without  relying  on  human
processing or client second-guessing.

For Clients (Risk Mitigation & Deliverable Control)
This is the total protection against project abandonment. Funds are locked safely in the smart
contract  and  are  only  released  when  verifiable  milestones  are  completed.  It  establishes  a
pay-for-performance pipeline for clients.

For Both Parties (Decentralized Governance & Cost Efficiency)
The  code  acts  as  the  ultimate  unbiased  escrow  agent  and  removes  traditional  third-party
banking  intermediaries.  By  automating  settlement  rules  via  Solidity  code  rather  than
intermediary  institutions,  transaction  costs  can  be  drastically  reduced  compared  to  the
10%–20% commissions charged by legacy platforms.

4.4 Customer Relationships
The relationship infrastructure is built heavily on programmatic fairness and transparency. It
will  also  show  how  the  platform  builds,  scales,  and  maintains  trust  automatically  between
clients and freelancers.

Automated & Trustless Self-Service
The  relationship  is  fundamentally  governed  by  code.  Users  do  not  need  to  "trust"  the
Milestone  Fund  team.  Instead,  they  trust  the  audited,  open-source  smart  contract executing
exactly as written. This has minimized the risk of administrative bias or server manipulation.

On-Chain Reputation System
A  transparent,  tamper-resistant  system  ledger  that  records  successful milestone transactions
directly onto the public blockchain to verify the credibility of both clients and freelancers.

Algorithmic Dispute Resolution
When  a  milestone  clarity  issue  arises,  the  platform  provides  a  structured,  multi-sig  escrow
dispute  mechanism  involving  platform-approved  neutral  arbitrators  to  fairly  evaluate
evidence.

4.5 Revenue Streams
MilestonePay adopts a low-margin, high-volume monetization strategy instead of relying on
high intermediary commissions. The platform generates revenue through four main streams:

Smart Contract Deployment & Settlement Fees
Small  transaction fees are charged when clients create project contracts and when milestone
payments  are  released  to  freelancers.  This  supports  automated  escrow  execution  through
predefined smart contract rules (Singh et al., 2025).

Dispute Arbitration Fees
When  disputes  require  intervention  from administrators or external arbitrators, a percentage
fee is deducted from the disputed escrow amount. This supports structured conflict resolution
in decentralized systems (Kovalchuk et al., 2025).

Premium Feature Subscriptions
Advanced tools such as tax compliance modules, premium contract templates, and automated
accounting features are offered through subscription plans.

Custom Enterprise Template Fees
 Corporate clients can purchase customized and pre-audited smart contract templates for large
or complex projects, improving deployment flexibility (Gans, 2020).

4.6 Key Resources
To  build  and  maintain  a  secure,  automated  escrow  ecosystem,  MilestonePay  relies  on  the
following key technical and human assets:

Blockchain Infrastructure
Public layer-1 or layer-2 networks provide the decentralized network environment to deploy
and  run  the  smart  contracts.  This  foundation  ensures  data  distribution,  immutability,  and
network longevity (Singh et al., 2025).

Audited Solidity Smart Contracts
A secure codebase built with robust access controls and standard logic patterns handles token
deposits,  escrow  management,  and  automated  milestone  payouts.  This  deployment  setup
builds trust through unchangeable conditions (Kovalchuk et al., 2025).

Decentralized Storage (IPFS)
Distributed  off-chain  storage  layers  are  used  to  store  user  profile metadata, milestone files,
and cryptographic deliverable proofs without single-point-of-failure risks.

Human Capital
A  core  team  of  full-stack  Web3 developers, UI/UX designers, and platform dispute officers
maintain the dApp frontend and handle escalated arbitration claims.

4.7 Key Activities
Key  Activities  refer  to  the  main  operations  required  to  ensure  that  MilestonePay  functions
effectively.  These  activities  support  the  platform's  value  proposition  by  providing  secure
payment  processing,  project  monitoring,  and  dispute  management  between  clients  and
freelancers.

Smart Contract Development
Smart  contracts  are  the  foundation  of MilestonePay. They are responsible for locking client
funds in escrow, managing milestone approvals, and automatically releasing payments when
predefined conditions are met (Buterin, 2014). Regular testing and maintenance are required
to ensure that the smart contracts function correctly and securely.

Escrow Management
MilestonePay uses an escrow mechanism to protect both clients and freelancers. Client funds
are securely held until the agreed project milestones are completed and approved. This helps
reduce payment risks and increases trust between both parties.

Milestone Verification and Payment Processing
The  platform  monitors  project progress through milestone submissions. Once a milestone is
completed, the client reviews the work and verifies whether it meets the project requirements.
After  approval,  the  smart  contract  automatically  releases  the  corresponding  payment  to the
freelancer.

Dispute Resolution
Disputes  may  occur  when  clients  and  freelancers  disagree  on  project  requirements,  work
quality,  or  milestone  completion.  In  such  situations,  platform  administrators  or  appointed
arbitrators  review  the  evidence  provided  by both parties and make a fair decision regarding
payment distribution.

User Verification
User  verification  helps  reduce  fraud  and  improve  platform  security.  Both  clients  and
freelancers  are required to register and verify their identities before using the platform. This
creates a safer and more trustworthy working environment.

Platform Maintenance
Regular  platform  maintenance  is necessary to ensure system stability, transaction reliability,
and  overall  platform  security.  Activities  include  monitoring  transactions,  fixing  technical
issues,  updating  system  components,  and  maintaining  overall  platform  security  and
performance (Mougayar, 2016).

4.8 Key Partnerships
MilestonePay  functions  as  an  open  infrastructure  layer  that  requires  solid  strategic
partnerships to ensure security, liquidity, and user adoption:

Blockchain Network Foundations
Technical collaboration with protocol entities like the Ethereum Foundation or Polygon Labs
provides developer support, network scalability, and optimization against gas fee spikes. This
ensures systemic longevity across distributed networks (Singh et al., 2025).

Fiat-to-Crypto Payment Gateways
Partnerships with payment providers, e-wallets, and banking networks handle initial deposits
and smooth out-ramp withdrawals for users unfamiliar with crypto. This expands marketplace
integration across traditional financial channels (Kovalchuk et al., 2025).

Independent Dispute Arbitrators
Alliances with certified legal mediators and project verifiers who act as unbiased third parties
resolve milestone conflicts fairly when escalated.

Security Auditors & Dev Communities
Independent  smart  contract  auditing  firms  and  open-source  communities  test  the  contract
logic  and  patch  any  security  vulnerabilities  before  deployment.  This  minimizes  overall
operational vulnerabilities (Singh et al., 2025).

4.9 Cost Structure
Blockchain Transaction & Deployment Fees
Network  gas  fees  are  incurred  when  deploying  smart  contracts, making proxy updates, and
recording milestone states on the blockchain ledger. This remains a key cost factor in public
ledger solutions (Gans, 2020).

Core Software Engineering & UI Maintenance
Ongoing development expenses maintain the React frontend dApp, fix bugs, and keep Web3
wallet integrations updated.

Legal Compliance & Regulatory Costs
Capital spent on legal consultations and compliance frameworks meets regional gig-economy
payment  regulations.  This  resolves  legal  uncertainties  surrounding  blockchain  deployment
(Kovalchuk et al., 2025).

Marketing & User Acquisition
Marketing  expenses  acquire  both  freelancers and clients through targeted ad campaigns and
community outreach to grow the two-sided marketplace.
